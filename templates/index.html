Sign language is a visual language that uses gestures, facial expressions, and body language to convey meaning. Each sign language has its own unique grammar and vocabulary. For example, American Sign Language (ASL) is widely used in the United States and Canada. It is essential to recognize that sign languages are not simply a manual representation of spoken languages; they have their own syntax and structure.

Our mission is to bridge the communication gap between hearing and non-hearing individuals through innovative technology. This project focuses on translating written text into sign language, making communication more accessible for everyone. Explore our website to learn more about our goals, technology, and the impact we aim to achieve.

About the Project

The Text to Hand Sign project is dedicated to developing systems that convert written text into sign language. By utilizing advanced technologies, we aim to empower individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing, enhancing their ability to communicate effectively in various settings.

Accessibility:

Our project promotes inclusivity by providing tools that help individuals with hearing impairments engage with the world around them.

Education:

We strive to create educational resources that facilitate the learning of sign language, benefiting both deaf and hearing individuals.

Community Impact:

By fostering better communication, we aim to strengthen relationships and understanding between diverse communities.
